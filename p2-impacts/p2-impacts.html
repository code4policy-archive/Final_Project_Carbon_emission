Beware of warmer summers and winters ahead!

All U.S. States will experience warmer summers and winters from 2020-2039 compared to 1981-2010


During the winter, the greatest impacts will be felt in Alaska, Northern New England, the Great Lakes region, and the Dakotas. Higher temperatures may impact businesses whose livelihoods depend on severe winters, such as ski resorts, snow removal companies, and heating contractors.

During the summer, one tangible impact will be to the potato-growing region of the United States. In Idaho, temperatures are expected to increase by 2.6 degrees. One iconic potato variety commonly used for french fries, the Russet Burbank, is extremely sensitive to temperature changes. Even small increases in environmental temperature can cause a discloring of fries made from the potatoes.


Interestingly, an increase in average temperatures would overall benefit energy use across most states. In future decades, warmer winters will decrease the need for heating, reducing energy consumption. Significant decrease in energy costs are evident in areas with notoriously harsh winters, such as Alaska, Maine, Colorado, and Ohio.

Under a longer term scenario, the eastern seaboard, Florida panhandle, and parts of the Southeast bordering the Gulf of Mexico will experience coastal damage from climate change.
